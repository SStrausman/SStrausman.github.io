Honestly the last time someone asked me for advice was when my friend was thinking about applying to dev bootcamp and she wanted to know if I thought she would be a good fit.

As I said in my stereotype threat blog post, I feel like this is less an issue for me than it is for other people. Part of my privilege as a white straight cis male is that people generally have favorable expectations of me. I do think that the things I mentioned (and other values I hold, obviously) do help me try to fight stereotyping that I might do that would contribute to other peoples senses of unease.

As for feeling down, my roommate tells me that she thinks my biggest issue with regards to dev bootcamp is impostor syndrome. I don't trust myself to be right and I get down easily with regards to not being able to figure something out. I need to be able to trust myself more often, and that is probably related to self respect, fairness (to myself) and other things not discussed.
